Who We Are

We are members of the Swarthmore Community organizing for improved labor conditions, such as higher wages, improved working conditions, and better employee benefits, at Swarthmore College. As a hub for labor organizing activity at Swarthmore College, Solidarity at Swat will advocate and organize for students, staff members, and faculty who want want to see improved labor conditions. We believe it is unacceptable for Swarthmore College to pay less than a living wage to any of its workers or deny benefits and services, such as health insurance or child care. If the administration chooses to hide behind technicalities and bureaucracy, we aim to force their hand. There is room for all who labor in Solidarity at Swat. Join us and fight for a world that realizes a guiding truth: "all labor has dignity" - Martin Luther King Jr.

We recognize that the exploitation of labor is intertwined with other forms of exploitation and oppression, and that a labor movement is incomplete without a commitment to support all struggles for justice. Injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere, and we stand in solidarity with struggles which are anti-imperial, anti-colonial, feminist, anti-racist, and for justice in general.

Current Goals

We are currently organizing towards a 15 dollar minimum wage for student workers. As our organization grows, current campaigns will be augmented and more campaigns will be added.

Our History and Work

Solidarity at Swat was formed after an inspirational talk by John Braxton '70 in April 2022. During this talk organized by the Swarthmore College Young Democratic Socialists of America, Braxton described his lifetime of organizing at the intersection of peace, labor, and the environment. After his talk, he asked some students about what issue unites the entire student body, and thus began the campaign for a $15 student worker minimum wage. With intention to build mass coalitions amongst various student groups, the Young Democratic Socialists of America established Solidarity at Swat as a coalitional organization for labor issues at Swarthmore.
